Accepting Students

The HAbits lab is seeking students with strong interest and competency in some combination of the following areas: mobile health, big data, embedded systems, machine learning, human-computer interaction, and wireless sensors.

Research

Our research aims to understand and improve people’s habits through data analytics and sensing in hopes of improving quality of life, preventing hospitalization and reducing healthcare costs.

Why Habits Lab?

What We Do

We design, build and analyze end-to-end mHealth systems, while focusing on processing its data to help answer health-related questions. We focus on signal processing and machine learning techniques to process time-series data generated from passive sensors. To advance existing sensing techniques, a part of our lab focuses on the design of embedded systems.

What We Vision

We envision a world where health costs and health care is manageable both in the U.S. And worldwide, where the majority of society can live a fulfilling life, and where individuals can achieve their full potential. We envision the advancement of technology to be used to help people regain control of their health problems and behaviors, leading them towards a balanced healthy lifestyle they can maintain.

Who We Are

We are creating a two-way bridge between computer science and preventive medicine and seeing what we get.
What They Say?

It is the humanity within us and the desire to improve quality of life and healthcare costs that guide our solutions to the persisting health problems of our time through computer science and behavior science based research in passive sensing data analytics; helping us advance our ability to understand, detect, predict, and ultimately prevent problematic health habits. We are the Health Aware bits (Habits) Lab.
