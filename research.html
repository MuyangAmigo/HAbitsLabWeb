Passive Sensing Data Analytics

With the ultimate goal of encouraging good eating habits and preventing poor ones, we have created a multi-sensor device which can detect and analyze eating events, including duration of an eating session, swallows, and whether the user is alone.

This will allow us to enumerate eating periods and extract features that are the first of their kind in this field.

Clustering Overeating in Obesity

Obesity, caused primarily by overeating, is a preventable chronic disease yielding staggering healthcare costs.

With the ubiquitous nature of wrist-worn sensors, existing literature has focused on detecting eating episodes in hopes of aiding users in dietary recall. We use wearable sensors, not only to aid in dietary recall, but to objectively identify problematic eating episodes.

We hypothesize that during an eating episode, eating duration as well as the number of feeding gestures, swallows, and chews are important physical features that can characterize overeating.

This effort focuses on a framework for characterizing challenging eating episodes through feeding gestures using wrist-worn inertial sensors.

Overeating Through The Virtual Lens of Passive Sensing

Overeating is one of the causes of obesity that can be prevented by identifying the essential factors that contributes to this unhealthy eating behaviour. The multiple factors that co-occur with overeating vary across individuals, making detection and prediction of this habit a great challenge for passive sensors, and an exciting area of research for our lab.

In this research we are combining machine learning and statistical methods to define problematic overeating behaviors in obese populations using both ecological momentary assessments and passive sensors. We are using clustering techniques to cluster overeating episodes into theoretically meaningful and clinically known problematic behaviors related to overeating.

This then will enable us to identify the essential factors that detect and even predict overeating, and ultimately provide personalized care that supports maintenance of healthy eating behaviors.
